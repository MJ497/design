Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our programs and application processes.

To apply for our scholarships:

  1. Register for an account on our scholarship portal
  2. Complete your profile with academic records and personal information
  3. Select the scholarship you wish to apply for
  4. Upload required documents (transcripts, recommendation letters, etc.)
  5. Submit your application before the deadline

You can track your application status through your portal account.

Eligibility varies by scholarship program, but general requirements include:

  • Nigerian citizenship or permanent residency
  • Proof of financial need (for need-based scholarships)
  • Minimum academic performance (varies by program)
  • Admission or enrollment in an accredited institution
  • Completion of all application requirements

Specific criteria for each scholarship are listed on the program details page.

Communities can benefit through:

  • Nominating your community for our quarterly medical outreach programs
  • Requesting malaria prevention education and net distribution
  • Partnering with us for school health initiatives
  • Participating in our maternal health workshops

Contact our health programs coordinator at health@auf.org.ng to discuss opportunities.

Yes! We welcome volunteers for:

  • Food packing and distribution events
  • Agricultural training programs
  • School feeding program support
  • Community outreach and education

Volunteers must complete an application and orientation process. Visit our volunteer portal to get started.

Our selection process is rigorous and merit-based:

  1. Initial screening for eligibility and completeness
  2. Academic record evaluation by our education committee
  3. Review of personal statements and recommendation letters
  4. Interviews for finalists (for some programs)
  5. Final selection by the scholarship committee

All decisions are based on established criteria and are final.
